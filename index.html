Project: Influence of news media on stock¶

        by Amara Midouhas and Rehan Mullan

Collaboration Plan:¶

Our goal is to answer the question, " How has AI in the media impacted technology stocks?"

The news plays a major role in shaping reader's perceptions. With AI becoming a popular area of discussion, authors have unitentionally displayed either their pessimism or optimism about AI. Is this bias affecting how readers are viewing technology companies ? In more depth, is the demand for stock in technology companies increasing or decreasing in accordance to media's perspective of AI?

We are using LiveShare for VSCode and met everyday for the past week for a couple of hours to work on the tutorial together. We are going to be using python libraries such as pandas, matplotlib, seaborn and jupyter labs as tools to help us complete this analysis. We worked on tidying the datasets togther, though Rehan did most of the graphs and Amara wrote most of the tutorial. So far in our analysis, we believe that the datasets we do have (The New York times sentiment analysis and the S&P 500 companies - Facebook, Amazon, AMD, Microsoft, and IBM) will support us in our analysis. The S&P 500 companies dataset does not include Google nor Tesla, so we want to include a historical stock dataset of Google and Tesla.

Dataset 1: Sentiment Analysis of the New York Times (https://msropendata.com/datasets/feed8996-d9d4-47b9-9938-44ed298628fc)¶

This dataset from Microsoft contains multiple articles for over 20 years. We believe this dataset is appropriate for our analysis because the New York Times is one of the most popular newspapers read in the United States. Therefore, their perceptions play a major role in any major topic. The dataset includes the common words associated with AI like "Cyborg", "Singularity", and "Healthcare." These words and more are placed into a negative or positive column to judge the use of it. Additionally, there are columns of the date and time of when the article was published, it's section, title, and where AI was mentioned in the article. It also includes how revelant AI was to the article and the author's mood about it with a score of 5 being the highest. We believe this dataset is appropriate for our analysis because the New York Times is one of the most popular newspapers read in the world. Therefore, their perceptions play a major role in people's outlooks on certain topics.

How has the perceptions of AI changed overtime?

Here we were able to group all the common dates so that they can fit into one bin. We then plotted the sum of the "AI Mood" column. This graph helps us see the common trend in the mood of AI. A surprising outcome of this plot was that the mood of AI overtime has positively increased. Also, there was a huge spike in the 2000s.

Therefore, AI is starting to have a better outlook in the media's eyes.
